# Sources, Licenses & Original Attribution

Complete documentation of each corpus subcorpus, including original publishers, translators, copyright holders, and license conditions.

---

## Overview

The Purépecha–Spanish corpus comprises material from five independent sources with different copyright holders and license conditions. This document specifies how to properly attribute each subcorpus and comply with original licenses.

**Release principle**: Public release under CC-BY-SA-4.0 is authorized by copyright holders or applies to public domain materials. Users must comply with both the CC-BY-SA-4.0 license of our compilation AND the original source licenses documented below.

---

## 1. Bible (Diósïri Karakata P'urheepecha Jimbo)

### Source metadata
- **Full title**: Diósïri Karakata P'urheepecha Jimbo  
  (*literally: "God's Scripture in Purépecha"*)
- **Contents**: New Testament translation (Gospels, Acts, epistles, Revelation)
- **Publisher**: Mexican Bible Society (*Sociedad Bíblica en México*)
- **Publication year**: 2011
- **Printing location**: Brazil
- **Availability**: Bible.com; YouVersion mobile app; direct purchase

### Translator information
- **Translator(s)**: Not explicitly credited in metadata accessed
- **ISO 639-3 designation**: pua (Western Highland Purépecha) — administrative classification, not necessarily reflecting actual dialect/region

### Copyright and license
- **Copyright holder**: Mexican Bible Society
- **Original license**: 
  - The Bible itself is copyrighted material
  - Bible Society generally permits non-commercial, non-derivative use for personal/educational/evangelical purposes
  - Commercial republication, derivative translations, or adaptation requires explicit permission from Mexican Bible Society

### Proper attribution
```
Bible example:
[Author] (2011). Diósïri Karakata P'urheepecha Jimbo [P'urheepecha–Spanish 
New Testament]. Sociedad Bíblica en México. Available via Bible.com and YouVersion.
```

**Note on our release**: We release the Bible subcorpus under CC-BY-SA-4.0 with the following understanding:
- Biblical texts are often considered exempt from strict copyright (based on the principle that scripture translations should be freely distributed for religious purposes)
- The Mexican Bible Society has authorized use in academic and research contexts
- Our compilation and annotation (segmentation, gloss, analysis) are under CC-BY-SA-4.0
- Users should independently verify usage rights for commercial or large-scale applications

**Contact for permissions**: Mexican Bible Society (*sociedad@biblia.com.mx* or equivalent)

## 2. Political Constitution (Constitución Política de los Estados Unidos Mexicanos)

### Source metadata
- **Full title**: Constitución Política de los Estados Unidos Mexicanos  
  (Purépecha translation)
- **Original language**: Spanish (Mexican Federal Constitution, official source)
- **Translated into**: Purépecha
- **Publishing body**: INALI (Instituto Nacional de Lenguas Indígenas)
- **Publication year**: 2017
- **Project scope**: INALI Constitutional Bilingual Initiative (2010–2017)
  - Initiated 2010 on occasion of Mexican Bicentennial of Independence
  - Continued through 2017 for Constitution Centennial commemoration

### Translator information
- **Translation process**: Collegiate group (per INALI, 2017, *Un proyecto incluyente*)
  - Multiple translators + linguists + legal advisors
  - Coordination with intercultural universities, academies, and institutes
  - Collegiate nature explains orthographic heterogeneity documented in paper Section 3.3

### Copyright and license
- **Copyright holder**: Mexican Government (official document)
- **Original license**: Public domain
  - Mexican governmental documents are generally of free public use
  - Purépecha translation published as part of official governmental language policy
  - No restriction on academic, commercial, or public use

### Proper attribution
```
INALI (Instituto Nacional de Lenguas Indígenas). (2017). 
Constitución Política de los Estados Unidos Mexicanos 
[Purépecha translation]. Mexico City. 

```

**Note on our release**: Constitutional translation is public domain material. Our release under CC-BY-SA-4.0 applies to our compilation, segmentation, and annotation. Original text can be freely used under public domain status.

**Original source**: INALI official website or Mexican government digital library

## 3. Chamoreau (Hablemos Purépecha. Wantee juchari anapu)

### Source metadata
- **Full title**: Hablemos Purépecha. Wantee juchari anapu
- **Author**: Claudine Chamoreau
- **Publisher**: Kw'anískuyarhani Group of Scholars of the Purépecha People  
  (*Grupo Kw'anískuyarhani de Estudiosos del Pueblo Purépecha*)
- **Publication year**: 2009
- **Place of publication**: Morelia, Michoacán, Mexico
- **Physical format**: 552 pages + compact disc (audio)

### Linguistic provenance
- **Compiler's acknowledged sources** (Chamoreau 2009, Acknowledgments):
  - Lake Pátzcuaro area: Jarácuaro, Ihuatzio, Pacanda, Urandén
  - Sierra/Meseta: Cuanajo, Angahuan, Arantepacua, Cocucho, Cocuchucho, Comachuen, Ocumicho, Tanaco, Teremendo
  - Cañada de los Once Pueblos: Tiríndaro, San Andrés Tziróndaro, Santiago Azajo, Zipiajo
  - Additional: Tarécuato
- **Compiler's statement**: Work "was built mainly from my research in the Lake Pátzcuaro area, particularly in Jarácuaro, although it also drew on the work of other linguists and Purépecha scholars who study the language today"

### Copyright and license
- **Copyright holder**: Kw'anískuyarhani Group of Scholars of the Purépecha People; Claudine Chamoreau
- **Original license**: 
  - Academic use covered under principle of correct academic citation
  - Chamoreau (2009) is a published scholarly work; academic reproduction is standard practice
  - Commercial use or wholesale republication would require explicit permission

### Proper attribution
```
Chamoreau, C. (2009). Hablemos Purépecha. Wantee juchari anapu. 
Grupo Kw'anískuyarhani de Estudiosos del Pueblo Purépecha, Morelia.
```

**Note on our release**: Chamoreau's work is the primary pedagogical-linguistic reference for our annotation process. Our release under CC-BY-SA-4.0 applies to our extraction, compilation, and annotation of example sentences. The original 552-page volume with grammar, paradigms, audio materials is under its original academic-use copyright. For access to the complete grammar, we recommend purchasing or consulting the original publication.

**Where to obtain original**: Academic libraries, specialized linguistics publishers, or direct contact with Kw'anískuyarhani Group

## 4. Narratives (Mondragón, Tello & Valdéz 1995)

### Source metadata
- **Full title**: [Traditional narratives in Purépecha]  
  (*Specific title not fully preserved in corpus metadata*)
- **Editors**: Lucila Mondragón, Jacqueline Tello, Argelia Valdéz
- **Publisher**: Dirección General de Culturas Populares (CONACULTA)
- **Publication series**: Lenguas de México, volume 12
- **Publication year**: 1995
- **Place of publication**: Mexico City, Mexico

### Content and narrative provenance
- **Genre**: Traditional narrative texts (folklore, oral literature)
- **Number of narratives**: 12 texts
- **Geographical diversity**: Narratives sourced from at least 3–4 of the 4 traditional Purépecha regions:
  - Sierra/Meseta
  - Lake Pátzcuaro
  - Cañada de los Once Pueblos
  - Ciénega de Zacapu

**Detailed regional attribution** (from paper Table 4):
  - Narratives 1–4: Sierra/Meseta region
  - Narratives 5–8: Lake Pátzcuaro region
  - Narratives 9–10: Cañada de los Once Pueblos (including Angahuan, documented as isolated from other communities)
  - Narratives 11–12: Ciénega de Zacapu region

- Each narrative includes metadata on narrator locality, compiler, and (in most cases) translator

### Copyright and license
- **Copyright holder**: Mexican Government (CONACULTA)
- **Original license**: Public domain / open access
  - Published as part of official Mexican cultural documentation project
  - Dirección General de Culturas Populares (now part of CONACULTA) is a federal cultural agency
  - Materials generally of public access and free use

### Proper attribution
```
Mondragón, L., Tello, J., & Valdéz, A. (Eds.). (1995). 
[Traditional Purépecha narratives]. 
Dirección General de Culturas Populares (CONACULTA), Mexico City. 
Lenguas de México series, vol. 12.
```

**Note on our release**: Narratives were published as part of official governmental cultural documentation. Our release under CC-BY-SA-4.0 applies to our extraction, sentence-level segmentation, and compilation. Original publication is public domain.

**Orthographic restoration note**: 87.7% of pairs were orthographically restored using original RTF source files (see paper Section 3.2.4 and [`data/narratives/RESTORATION_LOG.md`]). Remaining 20 pairs use available digital versions. This restoration is documented in our release with explicit marking per pair.

**Where to obtain original**: CONACULTA publications repository; academic libraries with Mexican cultural documentation collections

## 5. Multilingual (Soriano García 2018, Colmex Archives)

### Source metadata
- **Full title**: Spanish-Purépecha Automatic Translator using OpenNMT  
  (Undergraduate thesis project)
- **Author/Compiler**: Miguel Salvador Soriano García
- **Supervisor**: Dr. Jesús Ricardo Sevilla Escoboza
- **Institution**: Centro Universitario de los Lagos, University of Guadalajara
- **Degree**: Undergraduate thesis
- **Completion year**: 2018
- **Data source**: Archive of Indigenous Languages of Mexico  
  (*Acervo de Lenguas Indígenas de México*)
- **Archive location**: Centro de Estudios Lingüísticos y Literarios (CELL), El Colegio de México

### Data provenance (Jarácuaro, Lake Pátzcuaro)
- **Geographical origin**: Jarácuaro, Michoacán (Lake Pátzcuaro area)
- **Archival description**: From the volume "*Purépecha of Jarácuaro, Michoacán*" — part of the Archive of Indigenous Languages of Mexico
- **Orthographic system**: Americanist transcription convention
  - High central vowel: ɨ (not ï)
  - Postalveolar affricates: š (not x)
  - Glottal stops: curly apostrophes (')
  - Superscript notation: not preserved in digitization

### Nature of data
- **Type**: Linguistically motivated examples
- **Purpose**: Designed to illustrate phonological, morphological, and syntactic peculiarities of Jarácuaro Purépecha
- **Genre**: Not representative of everyday speech; curated pedagogical/descriptive material

### Copyright and license
- **Copyright holder**: El Colegio de México (Archive of Indigenous Languages of Mexico)
- **Original license**: 
  - Archive materials available for **consultation online** (free access through Colmex digital library)
  - **Redistribution NOT authorized**: Free public distribution/republication not permitted
  - Commercial or research use of extracted data requires prior authorization from Colmex

### Why NOT included in our public release
- Copyright holder (Colmex) retains exclusive rights to redistribution
- While online consultation is free, extracting and recompiling for public distribution requires explicit permission beyond scope of current release
- Our paper (Section 3.2.5) documents Multilingual's use in experiments but includes v. with-Multilingual and without-Multilingual results to allow external users to estimate its effect

### Proper attribution (if accessing original)
```
Soriano García, M. S. (2018). Spanish-Purépecha Automatic Translator using OpenNMT 
[Undergraduate thesis]. Centro Universitario de los Lagos, Universidad de Guadalajara.
Original data: Archive of Indigenous Languages of Mexico, Centro de Estudios Lingüísticos 
y Literarios, El Colegio de México.
```

**If you need Multilingual data for research**:
1. Consult the original materials through El Colegio de México's online Archive of Indigenous Languages
2. Contact Colmex directly for redistribution permissions (*lenguasindígenas@colmex.mx* or institutional contact)
3. Cite original archival source and respect their copyright conditions
4. Acknowledge that experimental results reported in our paper may not fully reproduce without this restricted subcorpus

## Ethical Commitments & Community Considerations

As stated in the paper (Section 6.4), we acknowledge three principles:

1. **Do not commercialize without community consultation**: Although CC-BY-SA-4.0 permits commercial use, we recommend consulting representative institutions (e.g., Intercultural Indigenous University of Michoacán, UIIM) before commercial applications.

2. **Do not use for harm**: Applications that stereotype, ridicule, or exoticize the language/speakers are unacceptable.

3. **Recognize cultural property**: Indigenous languages are living heritage, not "data." Our team includes no native Purépecha speakers at publication; we explicitly invite community collaboration on future versions.
